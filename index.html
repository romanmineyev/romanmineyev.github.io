MACH2: Cancer Metastasis Prediction and Visualization

HTML, JavaScript, CSS, Cytoscape

Developed a website tool to visualize cancer metastasis, using a metastasis prediction algorithm called MACH2.

The MACH2 method systematically enumerates all plausible migration histories by exactly solving the PARSIMONIOUS MIGRATION HISTORY WITH TREE REFINEMENT (PMH-TR).

Published in RECOMB 2025.

Cellular Automata: The Game of Life

React, HTML, CSS, Javascript

Website for people to learn and play with cellular automata.

Cellular automata are computational models consisting of grids of cells that evolve over discrete time steps based on a set of rules determined by the states of neighboring cells. The "Game of Life" is a specific type of cellular automata, exhibiting complex and life-like behavior from a simple set of four rules.

About

I am a Master in Robotics student at the Georgia Institute of Technology. I currently work at The Laboratory for Intelligent Decision and Autonomous Robots (LIDAR) Lab on Bipedal Motion Planning, Control, and Social Navigation.

During my undergraduate years, I was a Mathematics & Computer Science major at the University of Illinois at Champaign-Urbana. I conducted 1.5 years of research at the Kinetic Intelligent Machines LAB (KIMLAB), where I contributed to a broad range of projects, ranging from autonomous chess playing with a robotic arm system to mobile-base manipulation.

My passion stems from a relentless curiosity and drive to improve the quality of human lives through robotics. This dedication motivates me to tackle complex challenges in robotics and contribute to advancements that can shape the future of humanity.
